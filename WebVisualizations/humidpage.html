Summary: Latitude vs. Humidity

Here we have the latitude vs humidity plot. This is quite an interesting plot and shows us some pretty interesting data about our planet and humidity. As expected, the equator, which as we can see from the latitude vs temperature plot, tends to record higher humidity levels. In the southern hemisphere, it generally tends to stay more humid with a not much of a dropoff as we get further away. Looking at the climates of these areas of the world, we tend to see more of the rainforests in these areas.

Moving north of the equator is some what of a different story. From our data we can see that rainforests extend past the equator for before reaching what looks like a much dryer zone. Going back to our latitude vs temperature chart, we know this is still hot area. Hot and dry, what does that bring to mind? Deserts! Extending from Mexico and the Southern United States to Africa and the Middle east, here we will find most of the worlds deserts. As we start to move further north we return a greener landscape, brings back the higher humidity readings.
