Summary: Latitude vs. Cloudiness

There isn't to much that we can assertain from the cloudiness plot. One thing that we can see is that the extreme ends of the globe seem to not have much cloud coverage at all. According to our data there are fairly clear skys around the poles. This might not necessarily mean that there are nothing but clear skys at the ends of the earth, but could be explained by a lack of data from these areas. The farther we go north and south the less and less population we see. We have a much larger population living closer to either side of the equator then the poles. This could leads to less cities to report data about cloudiness as we get closer to the poles.
